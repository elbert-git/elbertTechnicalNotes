Product Design (UX)

Previously known as UX

The big picture design of softwares. From research and project brief to delivery to further iterations. Consists of defining the issue to solve, prototyping with  wireframes and validating solution with quantitative and qualitative analysis.

Close relations with UI design

see ui design notes here. 
Often grouped in together with UI designers. Cause of the large overlap and tools these 2  fields use. 

The UX baggage. 

UX has a bad rep of being hocus pocus about their work. Often being frivolous and just being up their heads the whole time with their jargon. So best to be very objective and analytical with this field. Being data driven and backed up by tests. And focusing on solving the problem

The Mindset

UX came with the image of a bunch of hipsters with post-it notes and hocus pocus jargon that wow CEOs but don't actually design. While a lot of great things can be found in this field it's also littered with a bunch of useless practices. So here is the mindset you should have to avoid it al

Being data-driven

Use data and research to back-up your claims. 

User-focused and User respecting

Best to be not assume that you are the user. Actually take effort to listen to them and define the solutions around their problems and not the company goals.  

The Process

The UX process in brief

The process can vary between companies and designers. But they all follow the pattern of

  1. Discovering and Defining the problem
  2. Designing the Developing the solution
  3. Empirically Validating the solutions. 

Famous diagrams

Problem discovery  and definition

All about understanding the user's problem. Do your research. honestly unless you are in a large company, user interviews and surveys are usually enough. Then define the problem and solutions from the user perspective.  

Like from “Make Customers checkout items more” to “Create a better shopping experience for the user”

Designing Solutions 

Be fast and loose. Prototype with figma. have turnarounds measured in 1-2 days.
Also get references from competitors or similar apps. 

Validating the solutions

Again just interview should be enough.
